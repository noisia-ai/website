# Presentation 1 — Informative opener

**Use it for:** a lead who already knows Noisia and has talked to us before.
**Goal:** reconfirm the interest and make them want to book a first call.
**Length:** 9 slides · two versions (EN + ES).

> Slides 2, 3 and 8 carry industry/brand-specific copy. Replace `[BRAND]` and `[INDUSTRY]` with the real ones. The worked example under each slide shows the tone the platform should aim for when it auto-generates this (see automation note in the README).

> Most of what `[INDUSTRY]` needs to decide is already being said in public. Almost nobody is reading it on purpose.

### Slide 3 — What we already see in `[INDUSTRY]`
Three things the conversation makes obvious once you read it closely:

1. **The real objection isn't the one in the survey.** People say "price." What they mean is they don't trust the result enough to risk it.
2. **The competitor everyone watches isn't the one taking share.** Migration shows up in how people talk about switching, weeks before it shows up in numbers.
3. **The cultural permission is narrower than the brief assumes.** Some territories sound aspirational from the outside and read as "trying too hard" locally.

*(Keep 2 to 4. Pick the ones that hit `[BRAND]` hardest.)*

---

### Slide 4 — What we do at Noisia
We read public conversation and voice of customer (social, reviews, forums, search, marketplaces) and turn it into a decision your team can defend.

We're not a listening tool and not a dashboard vendor. We deliver the reading and the recommendation, with the evidence still attached.

---

### Slide 5 — What you get, and what it changes
Every study lands as an argument, not a pile of metrics:

- A clear thesis with the tension behind it
- A prioritized recommendation on what to do first
- The original sources behind each insight (text, date, platform, market)
- Reading cuts your team can act on this week

What it changes: you stop deciding on instinct and start deciding on evidence you can show the room.

---

### Slide 6 — How we work together
Three ways to bring us in:

- **One-time study** — the state of social conversation around a topic, category or brand.
- **Continuous monitoring** — reputation, competitors, crisis and industry, watched over time.
- **Audience and trends analytics** — consumer behavior, audiences and where the category is heading.

Studies start at 300 USD.

---

### Slide 7 — How we get started
1. **The real question.** We pin down the decision your team needs to make and what evidence would make it defensible.
2. **The right reading.** We pick the method and the sources that answer it (no generic dashboard).
3. **Clear scope.** Depth, sources, output and price, agreed before anything runs.

---

### Slide 8 — Studies we can run for `[BRAND]`
A short menu, all built on the same logic:

- Campaign launch: which story has real permission in the category
- Media optimization: where the message breaks before it converts
- Product development: which need is still badly solved
- Competitive defense: which competitor promise became more believable
- Trend anticipation: which signal is growing before it gets obvious
- Crisis decoding: what's actually driving a reputation moment

---

### Slide 9 — Closing
> The conversation is already happening. The only question is whether you're reading it before your competitor does.

Let's book 30 minutes.

### Slide 4 — Qué hacemos en Noisia
Leemos conversación pública y voz del cliente (redes, reviews, foros, búsquedas, marketplaces) y la convertimos en una decisión que tu equipo puede defender.

No somos una herramienta de listening ni un proveedor de dashboards. Entregamos la lectura y la recomendación, con la evidencia todavía pegada.

### Slide 5 — Qué recibes y qué cambia
Cada estudio llega como un argumento, no como un montón de métricas:

- Cortes de lectura que tu equipo puede accionar esta semana

Qué cambia: dejas de decidir por intuición y empiezas a decidir con evidencia que puedes mostrar en la junta.

Tres formas de entrarle:

- **Monitoreo continuo** — reputación, competidores, crisis e industria, vigilados en el tiempo.
- **Analítica de audiencia y tendencias** — comportamiento del consumidor, audiencias y hacia dónde va la categoría.

1. **La pregunta real.** Aterrizamos qué decisión necesita tomar tu equipo y qué evidencia la haría defendible.

3. **Alcance claro.** Profundidad, fuentes, salida y precio, acordados antes de correr nada.
